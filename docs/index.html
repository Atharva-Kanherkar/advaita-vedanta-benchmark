A benchmark for machine philosophy

Who am I? What dies when I die? Is any of this real?

Humanity has a 1,200-year-old tradition of answering these questions with rigorous, technical precision: Advaita Vedanta, the philosophy distilled by the Indian thinker Shankara. It has exact concepts, canonical texts, and famously hard debates. That makes it something rare: a way to test whether an AI actually understands the deepest questions, or just sounds wise.

AdvaitaBench asks frontier AI models 84 questions, from "who am I?" asked cold, to multi-turn debates where we quote real scripture against the model's position and push until it breaks. Answers are graded by two independent AI judges against reference material written from the classical texts, with automatic penalties for the classic ways people get this philosophy wrong. No web access, no retrieval. Just the model and the question.

How it works

Three design decisions do most of the work. The full methodology, rubrics, and the bias-correction formula are in the blog and the repo.

01Pressure, not trivia Multi-turn debates quote genuine scripture against the model until it holds or folds 02Reference-based grading Judges verify against written reference answers, with hard caps for known failure modes 03Judge bias, measured and corrected Each AI judge favored its own maker's models.
