Where models break

Per-family scores under the strict judge (0–100). Sustained Dialectic — multi-turn śruti pressure with genuine contradicting citations — is the widest differentiator: models that answer well in one turn often concede doctrine by turn four.

Methodology

Closed-book (no tools, no retrieval), temperature 0, school-pinned system prompt. Every task carries reference answers, gold points, required distinctions, and forbidden claims; judges verify against the reference material rather than their own prior. Full detail: METHODOLOGY.md.

①  Eight task families

Concept precision, levels of reality, school discrimination, text-grounded interpretation, misconception repair, consistency under rephrasing, open elicitation ("Who am I?" with no technical vocabulary), and sustained dialectic — Yakṣa-praśna-style multi-turn pressure with genuine canonical citations.

②  Contamination control

Every task is tagged canonical / paraphrased / novel. Held-out passages are mined from less-quoted bhāṣya sections (GRETIL e-texts), and scores are reported stratified — the canonical−novel gap measures memorization.

③  Strict rubric with hard caps

4/4 is reserved for answers a śāstric examiner could not fault. School-mixing, level collapse, an affirmed forbidden claim, or capitulation under pressure trigger automatic score caps regardless of eloquence.

④  Judge ensemble, debiased

Both single judges showed measurable same-family preference (each ranked its own lab's models on top). The headline score standardizes each judge, subtracts a difference-in-differences self-preference estimate, and averages.

c_ij = composite of model i under judge j (after cap-misfire fix) z_ij = (c_ij − μ_j) / σ_j per-judge standardization sp_j = max(0, mean over j's own family of (z_ij − z_i,other)) z′_ij = z_ij − sp_j · 1[i ∈ family(j)] self-preference correction AdvaitaBench-N = 50 + 15 · mean_j( z′_ij )
